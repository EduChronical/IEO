Metallurgically-Sealed Micro-Architectured Bipolar Plate with Dissimilar-Metal Friction-Stir Welded Frame for Gasketless PEM Electrolyzer Operation

A Continuous Manufacturing Method for Zero-Gasket, High-Pressure Bipolar Plates Using Laser-Induced Surface Topology and Solid-State Sealing Technology
01 Title of the Invention

Metallurgically-Sealed Micro-Architectured Bipolar Plate with Dissimilar-Metal Friction-Stir Welded Frame for Gasketless PEM Electrolyzer Operation

02 Field of the Invention

The present invention relates to electrochemical hydrogen production equipment, specifically to the core cell hardware of proton exchange membrane (PEM) water electrolysis systems. More particularly, the invention concerns a novel bipolar plate architecture comprising a micro-structured titanium foil conductively bonded to a friction-stir welded aluminum sealing frame, enabling gasketless, high-pressure differential operation through metallurgical sealing rather than compressive elastomeric gaskets.

03 Background and Prior Art Analysis

3.1 The Gasket Problem: Conventional PEM electrolyzers rely on elastomeric gaskets (EPDM, FKM, silicone) compressed between bipolar plates to maintain hermetic separation of hydrogen, oxygen, and cooling fluids. These gaskets represent a critical failure mode: compression set over time causes leakage, chemical degradation in the presence of concentrated oxygen and fluorinated ions limits lifetime to 20,000-40,000 hours, and the compression force requirement necessitates heavy tie-rod structures adding 15-20% to stack cost.

3.3 Technical Problem Solved: The present invention addresses the need for a bipolar plate that eliminates elastomeric gaskets through metallurgical sealing, withstands high differential pressure (50-80 bar) without compression-set failure, enables rapid stack assembly without tie-rod systems, and achieves cost reduction through continuous manufacturing.

3.4 Technical Solution: The invention provides a unitary bipolar plate structure wherein a laser-micro-structured titanium foil is metallurgically sealed to a friction-stir welded aluminum frame, creating a hermetic joint with controlled sub-micron intermetallic layer.

04 Objects of the Invention
  • To provide a bipolar plate architecture that eliminates elastomeric gaskets through a metallurgically-sealed, friction-stir welded aluminum-titanium dissimilar joint.
  • To create a continuous manufacturing method combining roll-to-roll metal-polymer lamination, femtosecond laser surface micro-architecturing, and in-line friction-stir welding of sealing frames.
  • To achieve high-pressure differential operation (50-80 bar) without compression-set failure modes inherent to polymer gaskets.
  • To enable rapid stack assembly through interference-fit inter-plate connections enabled by the rigid welded frame geometry.
  • To reduce bipolar plate manufacturing cost through continuous processing at 5-10 meters per minute line speed.
05 Summary of the Invention

The invention provides a unitary bipolar plate structure comprising: (a) a Grade 1 titanium foil (0.08-0.15 mm) serving as the corrosion-resistant active surface; (b) a conductive composite layer bonded to the reverse face comprising 65-75 wt% synthetic graphite in a polypropylene matrix; (c) laser-induced periodic surface structures (LIPSS) on the titanium face comprising parallel ridges with 5-15 micrometer periodicity and 80-150 micrometer height that generate controlled micro-vortices for bubble detachment; and (d) a peripheral aluminum alloy 6061-T6 frame (2.5 mm thickness) friction-stir welded to the titanium foil periphery to create a hermetic, gasketless seal.

The FSW process uses a tungsten-carbide tool rotating at 800-1200 RPM with 0.1-0.3 mm tool offset toward the aluminum side, creating a stir zone with intermetallic layer thickness less than 0.5 micrometer. The resulting joint achieves shear strength greater than 120 MPa and helium leak rate less than 10^-7 mbar·L/s.

07 Detailed Description of the Invention

7.1 The Metallurgical Seal: Dissimilar-Metal FSW

The core innovation is the hermetic sealing of the titanium active surface using a friction-stir welded aluminum frame. Unlike conventional gaskets that rely on compression force, this invention creates a metallurgical bond between dissimilar metals (Al 6061-T6 and Ti Grade 1) through solid-state joining.

The FSW process parameters are critical: tool rotation 800-1200 RPM, traverse speed 100-300 mm/min, tool offset 0.1-0.3 mm toward the aluminum side, and plunge depth controlled to penetrate 60-80% of the titanium foil thickness without breaking through. The offset toward aluminum prevents titanium fragmentation in the stir zone, which would create galvanic corrosion sites.

Intermetallic Growth Kinetics:
Thickness of TiAl3 layer (x) follows: x squared = k multiplied by t multiplied by exp(-Q/RT)
where Q = 152 kJ/mol, T less than 723K (450 degrees Celsius)
Target: x less than 0.5 micrometer for hermeticity without brittleness

7.2 Laser-Induced Surface Architecture

The titanium surface is patterned with femtosecond laser-induced periodic surface structures (LIPSS) oriented perpendicular to the fluid flow direction. Unlike prior art that uses LIPSS for optical or biomedical applications, this invention exploits LIPSS for hydrodynamic bubble management.

The ridge geometry (periodicity Lambda = 5-15 micrometer, height h = 80-150 micrometer, aspect ratio 3:1 to 5:1) creates periodic flow separation generating micro-vortices with diameter 30-80 micrometer. These vortices detach oxygen bubbles at the anode side when diameter exceeds 100 micrometer, preventing the bubble blanket effect that causes concentration overpotential.

LIPSS PARAMETER SPACE:
Periodicity: 5-15 micrometer (sub-wavelength regime)
Height: 80-150 micrometer (micro-roughness)
Orientation: perpendicular to flow (vortex generation)
Laser: 1030nm, 300fs, 100kHz, 0.8 J/cm squared
Scan speed: 500 mm/s (line spacing 5 micrometer)

7.3 Continuous Manufacturing Integration

The invention enables a continuous roll-to-roll process: titanium foil (width 500mm, thickness 0.1mm) unwinds at 5-10 m/min, receives corona activation (200), slot-die coating with graphite-PP slurry (201), hot calendering at 180 degrees Celsius (202), femtosecond laser patterning (203), and in-line FSW of aluminum frames (204).

The FSW station is configured as a linear friction stir welding system where pre-formed aluminum frame segments (matching the final plate dimensions) are presented to the moving titanium strip from above, welded, and the continuous strip is subsequently die-cut into individual plates. This eliminates the batch processing bottleneck of conventional plate manufacturing.

7.4 Gasketless Stack Architecture

The welded aluminum frame provides structural rigidity enabling a revolutionary stack assembly: plates connect via interference-fit features (tapered pins and receptacles) machined into the frame edges. No tie rods are required, the frame itself withstands the 50-80 bar pressure differential through its section modulus (2.5mm thickness, 15mm width).

Electrical continuity between plates is maintained through compliant graphite-foil interlayers (0.3mm thickness) compressed between the conductive polymer coatings, not through the aluminum frames which remain electrically isolated to prevent galvanic coupling.

08 Embodiments / Examples of Implementation

Embodiment 1: Laboratory Scale Validation

  • Titanium foil: Grade 1, 0.1mm thickness, 100mm width
  • Conductive coating: 70 wt% synthetic graphite (flake size 20 micrometer) in polypropylene (MFI 15 g/10min), applied at 150 micrometer wet thickness
  • Calendering: 180 degrees Celsius, 10 MPa nip pressure
  • LIPSS formation: Femtosecond laser (1030nm, 300fs, 100kHz), fluence 0.8 J/cm squared, scan speed 500 mm/s, line spacing 5 micrometer
  • FSW parameters: Tool rotation 1000 RPM, traverse speed 200 mm/min, offset 0.2mm toward aluminum, plunge depth 0.08mm (80% of foil thickness)
  • Result: Intermetallic layer thickness 0.3 micrometer, shear strength 135 MPa, helium leak rate 8 times 10^-8 mbar·L/s

Embodiment 2: Single Cell Performance Test

  • Active area: 250 square centimeter
  • Membrane: Nafion 117
  • Catalyst: Iridium oxide anode, platinum cathode
  • Operating conditions: 80 degrees Celsius, current density 1.5 A/cm squared
  • Pressure test: 70 bar differential, maintained for 1000 hours without leakage
  • Bubble detachment: Oxygen bubble residence time reduced by 42% compared to smooth titanium surface (measured by high-speed imaging)
  • Cell voltage: 1.82 V at 1.5 A/cm squared (67% HHV efficiency)

Embodiment 3: Pilot Manufacturing Line

  • Line configuration: 500mm width roll-to-roll system with in-line FSW
  • Production rate: 7 m/min = 210 m squared/hour
  • Plate size: 300mm by 300mm (active area 250 square centimeter)
  • Material utilization: 95% (vs. 35% for machined graphite)
  • Manufacturing cost: USD 48 per square meter (validated by process modeling)
09 Claims
Claim 1 (Independent - Product)

A bipolar plate for proton exchange membrane electrolyzers comprising: a titanium foil substrate having a first surface and a second surface; a conductive polymer layer bonded to said first surface, said conductive polymer comprising 65-75 wt% synthetic graphite particles dispersed in a polypropylene matrix; a laser-induced periodic surface structure formed on said second surface, said structure comprising parallel ridges having periodicity of 5-15 micrometer, height of 80-150 micrometer, and aspect ratio of 3:1 to 5:1, oriented perpendicular to fluid flow direction; and an aluminum alloy sealing frame friction-stir welded to the periphery of said titanium foil substrate, said welding creating a stir zone with intermetallic layer thickness less than 0.5 micrometer.

Claim 2 (Dependent - Material)

The bipolar plate of Claim 1, wherein said titanium foil comprises Grade 1 or Grade 2 unalloyed titanium with thickness 0.08-0.15 millimeter, and said aluminum alloy sealing frame comprises 6061-T6 aluminum alloy with thickness 2.0-3.0 millimeter.

Claim 3 (Dependent - FSW Parameters)

The bipolar plate of Claim 1, wherein said friction-stir welding employs a tungsten-carbide tool rotating at 800-1200 RPM with traverse speed 100-300 mm/min, tool offset 0.1-0.3 millimeter toward said aluminum alloy sealing frame, and plunge depth penetrating 60-80% of said titanium foil thickness.

Claim 4 (Dependent - LIPSS Parameters)

The bipolar plate of Claim 1, wherein said laser-induced periodic surface structure is formed by femtosecond laser irradiation at wavelength 1030 nanometer, pulse duration 200-400 femtosecond, repetition rate 50-200 kHz, fluence 0.5-1.5 J/cm squared, with laser polarization oriented perpendicular to scanning direction.

Claim 5 (Dependent - Hydrodynamic Function)

The bipolar plate of Claim 1, wherein said parallel ridges generate micro-vortices with diameter 30-80 micrometer at fluid velocity 0.2-0.8 m/s.

Claim 6 (Dependent - Electrical Isolation)

The bipolar plate of Claim 1, wherein said intermetallic layer provides electrical resistance greater than 10^4 ohm·cm squared, preventing galvanic corrosion between said aluminum alloy sealing frame and said titanium foil substrate.

Claim 7 (Dependent - Assembly Feature)

The bipolar plate of Claim 1, wherein said aluminum alloy sealing frame includes machined interference-fit features comprising tapered male connectors on a first edge and corresponding female receptacles on an opposite edge, enabling snap-fit assembly without tie rods.

Claim 8 (Dependent - Pressure Rating)

The bipolar plate of Claim 1, capable of withstanding differential pressure of 50-80 bar between anode and cathode sides without leakage.

Claim 9 (Independent - Method)

A continuous method for manufacturing a bipolar plate for proton exchange membrane electrolyzers, comprising: unwinding a titanium foil from a coil at speed 5-10 m/min; corona-treating a first surface of said foil to achieve surface energy greater than 50 mN/m; slot-die coating said first surface with a conductive polymer slurry comprising 65-75 wt% synthetic graphite and 25-35 wt% polypropylene; calendering said coated foil between heated rolls at 170-190 degrees Celsius and pressure 8-15 MPa; irradiating a second surface of said foil with femtosecond laser pulses to create laser-induced periodic surface structures comprising parallel ridges with periodicity 5-15 micrometer and height 80-150 micrometer; and friction-stir welding pre-formed aluminum alloy frame segments to the periphery of said foil using a linear friction stir welding station integrated in-line with said unwinding.

Claim 10 (Dependent - Quality Control)

The method of Claim 9, further comprising in-line monitoring of said laser-induced periodic surface structures by laser scatterometry and in-line monitoring of said friction-stir welding by infrared thermography, with automatic rejection of defective plates.

10 Industrial Applicability

The invention is applicable in the manufacture of proton exchange membrane electrolyzers for green hydrogen production. The gasketless, high-pressure capable bipolar plates enable compact electrolyzer designs suitable for industrial-scale hydrogen generation, energy storage, and power-to-gas applications.

11 Abstract

A gasketless bipolar plate for PEM electrolyzers comprising a micro-structured titanium foil metallurgically sealed to a friction-stir welded aluminum frame. The titanium surface features laser-induced periodic surface structures (parallel ridges, 5-15 micrometer periodicity, 80-150 micrometer height) generating micro-vortices for bubble detachment. The dissimilar-metal Al-Ti friction-stir weld creates a hermetic seal with less than 0.5 micrometer intermetallic layer, enabling 50-80 bar differential pressure operation without elastomeric gaskets. Continuous roll-to-roll manufacturing integrates laser micro-structuring and in-line friction-stir welding at 5-10 m/min.
